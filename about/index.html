I am a Veterinarian and Zootechnician (CMVP 12434), co-founder and chief medical officer of Zoovet Travel, with thirteen years of clinical practice.

I graduated from Universidad Privada Antenor Orrego (UPAO) and am registered with the Veterinary Medical College of Peru under number CMVP 12434. I work on one principle: the visible sign does not cause the disease, it announces it. I diagnose in systems, not in symptoms —dermatology, endocrinology, internal medicine and nutrition read as a single process— and support every conclusion with verifiable evidence and open-access publications with DOIs.

In 2013 I co-founded Zoovet Travel, in Trujillo, Peru, a practice specialized in veterinary travel medicine and international pet export to more than twenty destinations: the same clinical logic applied to a documentary and sanitary chain that must hold from start to finish for an animal to travel safely.

I am a member of the Public Health Committee of the Departmental Veterinary Medical College of La Libertad. I am registered as a researcher in the National Researcher Directory CTI Vitae of CONCYTEC (ID 0140858, with data verified by RENIEC) and hold permanent academic identifiers —ISNI 0000 0005 3051 4034, ORCID 0009-0002-6837-5311, CMVP registration 12434, CTI Vitae CONCYTEC ID 0140858— as well as indexed presence in FAO AGRIS and Google Scholar. The organization I co-founded, Zoovet Travel, holds its own ISNI: 0000 0005 3051 3904.

My open-access work includes the Zoovet Travel Technical Series in International Companion Animal Transport Medicine (deposited on Zenodo/CERN under a CC BY 4.0 license) and clinical technical reviews with DOIs on canine endocrine-dermatological diagnosis. My systemic diagnosis consultation is also available remotely.
